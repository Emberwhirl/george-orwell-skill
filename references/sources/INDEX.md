# George Orwell — Primary Source Archive INDEX

Archive built 2026-07-20 for the george-orwell-skill research project. All files are English-language full texts of works by George Orwell (Eric Arthur Blair, 1903-1950). All files below are [PRIMARY] — Orwell's own words as transmitted by the named archive site.

## COPYRIGHT NOTE

Orwell died 21 January 1950. His works entered the **public domain in life+70 jurisdictions (UK, EU, Australia, Canada, Russia, etc.) on 1 January 2021**. However, **US copyright runs 95 years from first publication**, so the later books remain in copyright in the United States — *Animal Farm* (1945) until 2041 and *Nineteen Eighty-Four* (1949) until 2045, with other 1930s-40s works expiring on their own 95-year clocks. The source sites used here (Project Gutenberg Australia, orwell.ru) are hosted in life+70 jurisdictions. **This archive is for local research use only; do not redistribute the full texts from US-hosted services.** Diary and letter texts follow Peter Davison's editions and are reproduced online by the Orwell Prize/Orwell Foundation "Orwell Diaries" project and press reproductions; treat them the same way.

Notes on provenance:
- Files from "Fifty Orwell Essays" were programmatically split from the single Project Gutenberg Australia HTML ebook (0300011h.html, produced by Colin Choat) and converted to plain text; each file carries a source header.
- orwell.ru files were stripped from the site's HTML `read` div to plain text; footnote markers ("[back]") retained.
- gutenberg.net.au book .txt files are unmodified downloads (three are ISO-8859-1 encoded: Homage to Catalonia, The Road to Wigan Pier, Down and Out; use `grep -a` or convert with iconv).
- Every file was verified non-trivial and spot-checked for a signature phrase (e.g. "Big Brother", "P.O.U.M.", "Moulmein", "If liberty means anything at all").

## Not archived

- **The Orwell Foundation (orwellfoundation.com)** returned "Not Found" to non-browser clients for every essay URL tried; nothing was sourced from it directly (its Orwell Diaries WordPress project was reachable and used).
- No target work is missing: all 8 books, all 31 requested essays, the Willmett letter, As I Please samples, and wartime diary extracts were obtained. Items obtained from a fallback rather than the preferred site: The Freedom of the Press and the three orwell.ru-only essays (My Country Right or Left, Pacifism and the War, New Words) came from orwell.ru; the Willmett letter came from an Open Culture reproduction of the Davison edition rather than a manuscript source.
- Standalone gutenberg.net.au editions of Shooting an Elephant (0200141.txt) and Politics and the English Language (0200151.txt) exist but were not duplicated; the Fifty Orwell Essays versions are archived instead.
